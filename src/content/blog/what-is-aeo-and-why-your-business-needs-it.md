You've probably heard of SEO. Search Engine Optimization has been the standard way businesses get found online for over two decades. But there's a new acronym you should know: AEO, or AI Engine Optimization.

## How People Search Is Changing

More and more people are skipping Google entirely. Instead of typing a query and scrolling through ten blue links, they're asking AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity for direct answers.

"What's the best HVAC company in Pittsburgh?"
"Find me a web developer who works with small businesses."
"What's the difference between a static and dynamic website?"

When someone asks an AI these questions, the AI gives a direct answer. It doesn't show a list of websites. It recommends specific businesses by name. If your business isn't showing up in those answers, you're invisible to a growing segment of potential customers.

## What Is AEO?

AEO is the practice of optimizing your online presence so that AI systems understand, trust, and recommend your business. It's not a replacement for traditional SEO. It's an addition to it.

Think of it this way: SEO helps you rank on Google. AEO helps you get recommended by AI.

## How Does It Work?

AI systems pull information from across the web to form their answers. The sources they trust most include:

- **Your website content**: Clear, well-structured information about what you do, where you operate, and who you serve.
- **Reviews and testimonials**: What your customers say about you on Google, Yelp, and other platforms.
- **Structured data**: Technical markup on your website that helps AI understand your business details (location, services, hours, etc.).
- **Industry authority**: Are you writing about your field? Do other sites link to you? AI systems weigh authority heavily.

## What Can You Do Today?

Here are a few concrete steps to start building your AEO presence:

1. **Make sure your website clearly states what you do and where you do it.** Sounds obvious, but many business websites bury this information.

2. **Add structured data to your site.** This is technical markup (JSON-LD) that tells AI systems your business name, address, phone number, services, and more.

3. **Get more reviews.** AI systems trust businesses with consistent, positive reviews across multiple platforms.

4. **Publish helpful content.** Blog posts, guides, and FAQs that answer real questions in your industry. The more helpful content you have, the more likely AI systems are to reference you.

5. **Keep your business profiles updated.** Google Business, Yelp, LinkedIn, and industry directories should all have accurate, consistent information.

## This Isn't Going Away

AI-driven search is growing fast. The businesses that start optimizing for it now will have a significant advantage over those who wait. Just like the early days of SEO, the first movers get the biggest benefit.
