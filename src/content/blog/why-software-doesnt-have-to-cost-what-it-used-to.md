Five years ago, building a professional website for a small business meant paying $10,000 to $30,000. A custom web application could easily run six figures. Those prices made sense at the time because the work genuinely took that long.

That's not the case anymore.

## What Changed

AI development tools have fundamentally shifted how software gets built. Tasks that used to take days now take hours. Boilerplate code that used to be written line by line can be generated and refined in minutes. Testing, debugging, and documentation have all gotten faster.

This isn't about replacing developers with AI. It's about giving skilled developers better tools. A good carpenter with a power saw builds faster than one with a hand saw, but the quality of the work is the same. That's exactly what's happened in software.

## So Why Haven't Prices Dropped?

Most agencies haven't lowered their prices because their clients don't know any better. If you've never built software before, you have no way of knowing whether $25,000 is a fair price for a marketing website. You trust the agency to tell you what it costs, and they tell you what they've always charged.

That's the model we think is broken.
